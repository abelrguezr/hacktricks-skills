# Cookies Policy Generator

A skill for creating, reviewing, and improving cookies policies for websites.

## What this skill does

This skill helps you:
- Generate complete cookies policies for websites
- Review existing policies for completeness and compliance
- Understand different cookie types and their legal requirements
- Create cookie consent banners and notices
- Ensure compliance with GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy regulations

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when:
- Creating a cookies policy from scratch for a website
- Reviewing or improving an existing cookies policy
- Understanding what cookie types exist and their purposes
- Ensuring compliance with privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Creating cookie consent mechanisms or banners
- Explaining cookie practices to users or stakeholders

## Cookie types explained

### Essential cookies
These are necessary for basic website functionality like user authentication, security, and remembering preferences. They don't require consent under most regulations.

### Performance cookies
These help understand how visitors interact with websites by collecting anonymous information to improve performance and user experience.

### Functionality cookies
These remember user choices like language or region to provide personalized experiences.

### Targeting/advertising cookies
These deliver relevant ads and marketing communications based on user interests and browsing history. These typically require explicit consent.

## Policy structure

A complete cookies policy should include:

1. **Introduction** - What the policy covers and which websites
2. **What are cookies** - Basic explanation for users
3. **How we use cookies** - Detailed breakdown by type
4. **Third-party cookies** - External services and their policies
5. **Managing cookies** - How users can control cookies
6. **Changes to policy** - Update notification process
7. **Contact information** - How to reach the organization

## Generating a policy

When creating a cookies policy, gather this information:

1. Website name and URLs covered
2. Types of cookies used (essential, performance, functionality, targeting)
3. Third-party services that set cookies (analytics, ads, social media)
4. Organization contact information
5. Applicable jurisdictions (GDPR for EU, CCPA for California, etc.)

## Reviewing policies

When reviewing an existing policy, check for:

- Clear explanation of what cookies are
- Complete list of cookie categories used
- Third-party cookie disclosures with links to their policies
- Instructions for managing/disabling cookies
- Last updated date
- Contact information for questions
- Compliance with relevant regulations

## Example policy template

```
# Cookies Policy

Last updated: [DATE]

## Introduction

This Cookies Policy applies to [WEBSITE NAME] ("we", "us", or "our").

By using our website, you agree to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

## What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help make websites work, improve functionality, and provide personalized experiences.

## How we use cookies

1. **Essential cookies**: Necessary for basic functionality like authentication and security.
2. **Performance cookies**: Help us understand visitor interactions anonymously.
3. **Functionality cookies**: Remember your choices like language or region.
4. **Targeting cookies**: Deliver relevant ads based on your interests.

## Third-party cookies

We may use third-party cookies for analytics, advertising, and social media features. These are subject to their respective privacy policies.

## Managing cookies

Most browsers allow you to manage cookies through settings. You can block, delete, or limit cookies, though this may affect website functionality.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy periodically. Review this page for the latest information.

## Contact us

Questions? Contact us at [EMAIL ADDRESS]
```

## Compliance notes

### GDPR (EU)
- Requires explicit consent for non-essential cookies
- Must provide easy way to withdraw consent
- Must disclose purpose of each cookie category

### CCPA (California)
- Right to opt-out of sale of personal information
- Must disclose categories of data collected
- Must provide "Do Not Sell My Info" option if applicable

### Best practices
- Use clear, plain language
- Provide granular consent options
- Make it easy to manage preferences
- Keep policies updated
- Document consent where required
